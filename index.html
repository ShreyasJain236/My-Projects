A. P. J. Abdul kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam (1931–2015), known as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble family, he rose to prominence through his contributions to India’s space and defense programs, playing a key role in the development of the SLV-III, Agni and Prithvi missiles, and the Pokhran-II nuclear tests. A passionate advocate for education and youth empowerment, he envisioned a developed India through advancements in technology and rural progress, as outlined in his book "India 2020". Renowned for his humility and simplicity, Kalam inspired millions with his belief in dreaming big and working tirelessly. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while addressing students, leaving behind a legacy of innovation, inspiration, and patriotism.
